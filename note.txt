technique
1. Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT): Training the model on labeled input-output pairs (your data) to adapt its behavior.
2. LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation): Parameter-efficient fine-tuning by injecting small trainable adapters into specific layers.
3. QLoRA: Combines LoRA with quantized (e.g., 4-bit) models for even more efficient fine-tuning.
4. PEFT (Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning): General term for methods like LoRA, Prefix Tuning, and Adapters that only update a small subset of parameters.
5. RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback): Uses human preference data to further align the model’s outputs.
6. DPO (Direct Preference Optimization): Fine-tunes the model directly on preference data without reward modeling.
7. SFT + RLHF: A two-stage process—first supervised fine-tuning, then RLHF.
8. Adapter Tuning: Adds small adapter modules to the model and only trains those.
9. Prompt Tuning / Prefix Tuning: Only optimizes special prompt or prefix tokens, not the model weights.
